Environment Index

Data: JJM + PMKSY Watershed KPIs

Measures water access, soil moisture conservation, afforestation & farmer beneficiaries. Mean 0.122 — lowest of all 7 pillars, signalling acute environmental stress.

Formula: 0.5 × JJM + 0.5 × PMKSY

Pauri Garhwal achieves perfect 1.0; Bhandara district scores lowest at 0.214.
Education Index

Data: UDISE+ (all school levels)

Infrastructure sub-score (60%): library, water, electricity, ICT. Human sub-score (40%): teacher quality & female teacher share. Mean: 0.730.

Formula: 60% Infra + 40% Human Capital

Andaman & Nicobar and Kerala lead education infrastructure coverage.
Livelihood Index

Data: MGNREGA district performance

Employment (days/HH, wage rate), social inclusion (women, SC, ST persondays), governance efficiency (payment & completion ratio). Mean: 0.294.

GPs with NIL expenditure are inverted — higher admin engagement = higher score.
MSME Index

Data: Udyog Aadhaar Registration

Total MSME density (50%) + Micro/Small/Medium composition (50%). Mean: 0.214. Strong urban concentration; Gujarat leads with 39 Emerging districts.

Medium-enterprise share is rarest — strongest signal of industrial maturity.
Health Index

Data: District IMR & U5MR (Census 2011)

U5MR (45%) + IMR (45%) + gender mortality gap (10%), all inverted. Mean: 0.651.

Formula: 45% U5MR + 45% IMR + 10% Gender

Kerala shows near-zero gender mortality gap — model for equity-led health governance.
Governance Index

Data: Performance Grading Index (PGI)

Outcome, ECT, Infrastructure, Safety, Digital, Governance Process. Mean: 0.620.

PCA Weight: 27.16% — highest of all 7 pillars.

G_Index is statistically the largest contributor to variance in district resilience.

Technical Methodology
Index Construction & Statistical Framework
Transparent, replicable, and grounded in government data — designed for continuous policy monitoring.
Step 1 — Data Collection

7 national datasets · 50+ variables:

• JJM — Har Ghar Jal certification
• PMKSY — Watershed KPIs
• UDISE+ — School infrastructure, ICT
• MGNREGA — Employment, inclusion
• Udyam — MSME unit counts
• Census 2011 — Labour, demographics
• PGI — Governance scoring

Step 2 — Normalization

All indicators scaled 0–1 using Min-Max normalization:

X_norm = (X − X_min) / (X_max − X_min)

Negative indicators (IMR, marginal workers, degraded land) are inverted after normalization so higher always = better.

Missing values filled with column median.

Step 3 — Composite Index

Policy Weights — Expert-assigned, SDG priorities. Livelihood + Environment = 40%.

PCA Weights — First principal component loadings. G_Index (27.16%) + Edu (25.62%) dominate.

DSRI = Σ wᵢ × Indexᵢ

Step 4 — K-Means Clustering (k=3)
Data Quality & Limitations

District Matching: 37 matched via RapidFuzz; 27 unmatched (assigned median).

Census 2011: Labour data 10+ years old; post-COVID shifts not captured.

JJM: Reported vs certified discrepancy in low-verification states.

MSME: Self-declaration data — likely under-registration in tribal/rural areas.

Detailed Methodology — Pillar Construction
🌿 ENVIRONMENT INDEX

Source: JJM (Har Ghar Jal) + PMKSY Watershed Programme.
Formula: 0.5 × JJM_norm + 0.5 × PMKSY_norm
JJM sub-index includes % certified HGJ villages and functional tap connections. PMKSY captures watershed treated area (ha), beneficiary farmers, soil moisture conservation, and afforestation ha. Both are normalized independently, then equally weighted. Mean: 0.122 — reflecting acute under-coverage nationwide.

🎓 EDUCATION INDEX

Source: UDISE+ (all school levels).
Formula: 0.6 × Infra_sub + 0.4 × Human_sub
Infrastructure sub-score: library availability, drinking water, electricity, ICT labs. Human capital sub-score: pupil-teacher ratio (inverted), female teacher share. PCA weight 25.62% — 2nd highest — reflecting strong statistical differentiation across districts.

👷 LIVELIHOOD INDEX

Source: MGNREGA district performance data.
Employment (days/HH, avg wage rate), social inclusion (women persondays %, SC persondays %, ST persondays %), and governance efficiency (timely payment ratio, work completion ratio, GPs with NIL expenditure — inverted). Districts with high NIL-expenditure GPs are penalised, reflecting administrative failure.

🏭 MSME INDEX

Source: Udyog Aadhaar / Udyam Registration portal.
Formula: 0.5 × MSME_density + 0.5 × Composition_score
Density = total registered MSMEs per lakh population. Composition score weights micro (40%), small (40%), medium (20%) enterprises — medium share is rarest and most signals industrial maturity. Strong urban concentration; prone to under-registration in tribal belts.

🏥 HEALTH INDEX

Source: Census 2011 district IMR and U5MR tables.
Formula: 0.45 × U5MR_inv + 0.45 × IMR_inv + 0.10 × GenderGap_inv
All three components are inverted (lower mortality = higher score). Gender gap = |Male_IMR − Female_IMR| captures equity. PCA weight: 18.30%. Kerala's near-zero gender gap serves as the benchmark for equity-led health governance.

🛡️ VULNERABILITY INDEX

Source: Census 2011 workforce tables.
Composite of 6 indicators: marginal worker share (30%), non-worker share (20%), agricultural dependence ratio (15%), short-duration work (15%), female work participation — inverted (10%), SC/ST share in marginal workers (10%). Districts high on agri-dependence and marginal-work concentration score lowest. PCA weight: 19.58%.

⚖️ GOVERNANCE INDEX (PCA Weight: 27.16% — Highest)

Source: Performance Grading Index (PGI), Ministry of Education.
Six PGI domain scores are normalised and averaged: Outcomes & Governance (OG), Access (Enrolment & Retention), Infrastructure & Facilities, Equity, Governance Process, and Teacher Education & Training (ECT). The G_Index carries the highest PCA weight (27.16%), meaning governance process quality is the primary statistical driver of inter-district resilience variation. Districts with strong PGI tend to outperform across multiple pillars, reinforcing its role as a systemic lever.

Policy Advisory
Recommendations by District Type
Differentiated policy pathways for each of the three cluster typologies, grounded in pillar-level performance gaps.
🏆 For Resilience Leaders

Scale & Export the Model: Districts like Dakshina Kannada, Koraput, and Pauri Garhwal should be designated as Central Excellence Models under NITI Aayog's Aspirational Districts framework — for peer learning and replication.

Cross-State Mentorship: Match each Resilience Leader with 2–3 Vulnerability Hotspot districts in Bihar or UP for targeted technical assistance on MGNREGA governance, PMKSY planning, and PGI improvement.

Advance to Tier-2 Challenges: Since basic resilience is secured, these districts should focus on deepening MSME ecosystem, climate adaptation, and green energy transition under PM-Kusum and NWM.

Data Leadership: Invest in district-level real-time dashboards, open data portals, and evidence-based local governance to maintain and raise scores as new datasets become available.

🌱 For Emerging Districts

Pillar-Specific Convergence: Most Emerging districts have 1–2 weak pillars. Gujarat districts need environment improvement (JJM + PMKSY); Rajasthan districts need health equity focus. State governments should build pillar-specific convergence plans.

Environment Fast-Track: E_Index mean (0.122) is the lowest pillar. Emerging districts should prioritise JJM certification completion and PMKSY watershed registration to quickly improve their composite score.

State-Led MSME Expansion: Districts with strong labour base but weak MSME (e.g., Rajasthan's Barmer) should be prioritised for UDYAM Melas, formalisation drives, and cluster development centres.

MGNREGA Governance Reform: Reduce GPs with NIL expenditure through DBT integration, social audits, and block-level MGNREGA monitoring officers. Timely wage payment (within 15 days) must become a mandatory compliance metric.

⚠️ For Vulnerability Hotspots

Multi-Pillar Emergency Response: Bihar's bottom-5 districts (Gopalganj, Purba Champaran, Buxar, Araria, Sitamarhi) require simultaneous intervention across all 7 pillars. A dedicated district transformation task force under PM-JANMAN is recommended.

Enhanced Central Transfers: Use DSRI cluster classification to create a third tier of per-capita fund allocation under MGNREGA, Jal Jeevan Mission, and Samagra Shiksha — Hotspot districts should receive 1.4× the national average.

Governance-First Strategy: G_Index (PCA weight 27.16%) is the biggest driver of resilience. Deputing experienced IAS officers to Hotspot districts as District Development Commissioners with multi-sector mandates is critical.

Health & Education Baseline: Immediate deployment of mobile health units, immunisation drives, and Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya expansion in districts where IMR > 70 and female teacher share < 20%. These form the foundation for all other improvements.
